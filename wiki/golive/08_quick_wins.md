# Top 10 Quick Wins
## mellow.menu Go-To-Market Plan — Document 08

**These are the 10 highest-impact actions to execute in the first 2–4 weeks. Do these before anything else.**

---

## Priority Principle

The quick wins below are chosen because they either:
1. **Unblock everything else** (without them, other marketing activity is wasted)
2. **Generate disproportionate returns** for the time invested
3. **Create compounding assets** (a testimonial works forever; a single outreach email doesn't)

---

## Quick Win #1 — Walk into 10 Restaurants This Week
**What:** Identify 10 restaurants within walking/driving distance of where you are right now that match the Tier 1 ICP (tourist area, casual dining, independent). Walk in during off-peak hours (2–4 PM). Ask to speak to the owner. Give your 30-second pitch.
**Why this first:** The fastest, highest-quality path to your first paying customer is a conversation you have today. No website required. No marketing materials required. Just you and a restaurant owner.
**Target:** Book 3 conversations from 10 walk-ins.
**Time to execute:** 1 day
**Priority:** Critical
**Owner:** Founder

---

## Quick Win #2 — Send a "Personal Ask" Email to Everyone Who Knows You
**What:** Write a personal, honest email to every founder, investor, advisor, friend, and former colleague who knows you. Tell them what mellow.menu does, that you're looking for your first 5 restaurant customers, and ask: "Do you know any restaurant owners I should talk to?"
**Why this second:** Your warm network is your best source of qualified introductions. A warm intro has a 5–10x higher conversion rate than cold outreach. One good email to 200 people can generate 10 warm introductions in 48 hours.
**Template hook:** "I've spent the last 18 months building mellow.menu — a digital menu platform for restaurants. We're live, the DNS is live at mellow.menu, and I'm looking for the first 10 restaurants to work with. If you know any restaurant owners who might benefit from this, an introduction would mean the world."
**Time to execute:** 2–3 hours to write + send
**Priority:** Critical
**Owner:** Founder

---

## Quick Win #3 — Record a 2-Minute Product Demo Video
**What:** Open Loom (free), hit record, and demonstrate the core "wow" loop: take a photo of a restaurant menu, watch it import via OCR, translate it to French and Japanese, and show the QR code ordering flow. No editing required. Raw Loom is fine for Phase 1.
**Why now:** Every sales conversation, outreach email, and product hunt launch will be dramatically more effective with a demo video. "Click here to watch a 2-minute demo" converts at 2–3x the rate of text-only emails. This video will be embedded on the homepage and used in every outreach email.
**Time to execute:** 2–4 hours (record, re-record if needed, upload)
**Priority:** Critical
**Owner:** Founder

---

## Quick Win #4 — Build the Demo Booking Page (/demo)
**What:** Build the /demo landing page with the demo booking form (restaurant name, contact name, email, restaurant type) and Calendly integration. This is fully specced in docs/features/completed/homepage-demo-booking-feature-request.md.
**Why now:** Without this, there is no way to capture leads from any marketing activity. This is the conversion mechanism that all other activity points toward. It should be the first development task completed.
**Time to execute:** 3–5 days (developer)
**Priority:** Critical
**Owner:** Developer

---

## Quick Win #5 — Publish Terms & Conditions and Privacy Policy
**What:** Get a baseline T&C and Privacy Policy live on mellow.menu before any public-facing marketing. Use a lawyer or a reputable SaaS legal template service (Termly, iubenda, or a specialist SaaS lawyer). Ensure GDPR compliance for EU users.
**Why now:** Without this, you cannot legally take paying customers or collect marketing analytics data. Any press coverage that sends traffic to a site with no T&Cs or Privacy Policy will leave those visitors unable to trust the product.
**Time to execute:** 3–5 days (with lawyer review)
**Priority:** Critical
**Owner:** Founder

---

## Quick Win #6 — Update Your LinkedIn Profile to Reflect mellow.menu
**What:** Update your LinkedIn headline to "Founder @ mellow.menu — Digital menus + AI pricing + ordering for restaurants." Add mellow.menu as your current company. Write a short summary that explains what the product does and who it's for. This takes 30 minutes.
**Why now:** Every person who looks at your LinkedIn profile after seeing any of your outreach is going to check your profile. If it doesn't match what you pitched, trust drops. Also, LinkedIn's algorithm rewards profile completeness with more reach.
**Bonus:** Post a LinkedIn announcement this week: "I've been building mellow.menu for [X months]. Today it's live. Here's what it does and why I built it." This is your origin story post — your best chance for organic viral reach on LinkedIn.
**Time to execute:** 30 minutes for profile; 1 hour for the announcement post
**Priority:** High
**Owner:** Founder

---

## Quick Win #7 — Identify and Contact Your 5 Anchor Restaurant Candidates
**What:** Using the Tier 1 ICP criteria, identify 5 restaurants that are an ideal fit for mellow.menu. Prepare a personalised outreach message for each one. Contact them today — ideally by visiting in person (Quick Win #1) or by sending a short, specific email/DM.
**The Pilot Offer:** 3 months free on the Professional plan in exchange for feedback, attendance at a 30-minute onboarding call, and permission to use their name/quote in marketing.
**Why 5 specifically:** 5 anchor restaurants gives you enough usage data to find bugs, enough testimonials for a homepage, and enough real-world usage to make a Product Hunt launch credible. Don't launch publicly until you have at least 3 of these in place.
**Time to execute:** 2 hours to identify + personalise messages; ongoing follow-up
**Priority:** High
**Owner:** Founder

---

## Quick Win #8 — Set Up Google Search Console and Submit Your Sitemap
**What:** Verify ownership of mellow.menu in Google Search Console. Generate and submit an XML sitemap. Check for any crawl errors. Set up weekly performance email reports.
**Why now:** Google needs time to crawl and index your site. Every day you delay costs you indexing time. This takes 30 minutes and has compounding value — better indexing from Day 1 means earlier organic traffic.
**Also do:** Set up GA4 on the marketing site (30 minutes). Configure demo booking form submission as a conversion event. These are your core analytics tools and should be live before any traffic arrives.
**Time to execute:** 1 hour
**Priority:** High
**Owner:** Founder + Developer

---

## Quick Win #9 — Create a Simple One-Page Sales Leave-Behind
**What:** Create a single PDF (or beautifully formatted Notion page or landing page) that explains mellow.menu in plain language: what it does, who it's for, the 3 key benefits, pricing tiers, and how to get started. This is what you send after a walk-in conversation or demo call.
**Structure:**
- Headline: "mellow.menu — digital menus in 40+ languages, with AI pricing and table ordering"
- 3 benefit bullets with specific claims
- Pricing table (Starter / Pro / Business)
- "Get started at mellow.menu or email [your email]"
- A QR code linking to the demo booking page
**Why now:** Every in-person conversation (Quick Win #1) needs a follow-up asset. Without something to leave behind, the conversion window closes. This document will be used in every sales interaction.
**Time to execute:** 2–3 hours
**Priority:** High
**Owner:** Founder

---

## Quick Win #10 — Join and Engage in 3 Restaurant Owner Communities
**What:** Find and join the most active restaurant owner Facebook groups and LinkedIn groups in your target market. Spend 30 minutes per day for the first two weeks reading, commenting helpfully, and building presence. Do not promote mellow.menu yet — just be a helpful member first.
**Why now:** Community trust takes 2–3 weeks to build. If you join today and start contributing, you'll be in a position to share your product (appropriately, as a recommendation not an ad) in 3 weeks — right when your Phase 2 launch happens.
**Recommended communities:**
- Facebook: "Restaurant Owners UK", "Restaurant Owners Ireland", "Independent Restaurant Owners"
- LinkedIn: Search "restaurant owners group", "hospitality professionals"
- Reddit: r/restaurantowners, r/smallbusiness
**Time to execute:** 30 minutes to join; 30 minutes/day ongoing
**Priority:** Medium-High
**Owner:** Founder

## What Comes After the Quick Wins?
