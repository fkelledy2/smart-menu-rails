## Channel 1 — Founder Outbound (Direct Restaurant Outreach)

**Phase:** 1–3 (primary in Phase 1–2)
**Target audience:** Independent restaurant owners, Tier 1 ICP
**Messaging angle:** Peer-to-peer, direct, specific to their restaurant
**Expected CAC:** EUR 0–100
**Effort:** High (founder time)

### Strategy
The fastest path to paying customers is the founder personally reaching out to restaurant owners in the cities they know. This is not a "scale" play — it's a precision play. Every conversation is a customer discovery call.

### Specific Tasks

**Phase 1 (Weeks 1–4):**
- [ ] Build a list of 50 restaurants in your target city/cities that match the Tier 1 ICP profile
- [ ] Use Google Maps, Instagram, and local food blogs to find tourist-area restaurants with paper or static QR menus
- [ ] Walk into 10 restaurants in person this week and ask to speak to the owner (the in-person walk-in still has an extremely high conversion-to-conversation rate in hospitality)
- [ ] Send personalised email/LinkedIn DM to 40 additional targets (see email template below)
- [ ] Target response rate: 10–15%; meeting rate: 5–8%

**Phase 2 onward:**
- [ ] Systematise into a 5-touch outreach sequence (email → LinkedIn → email → call → final email)
- [ ] Aim for 50 outbound contacts per week by Month 3
- [ ] Track all outreach in the internal CRM (CRM feature is specced — see crm-sales-funnel.md)

### Outreach Email Template (Cold)
```
Subject: [Restaurant name] — your menu in 40+ languages, live in 30 minutes

Hi [First name],

I was in [area] last week and stopped by [restaurant name] — loved the [specific dish/detail].

I'm the founder of mellow.menu — a platform that gives restaurant owners a live digital menu 
their guests can read in their own language, order from, and pay through.

For a restaurant like yours that [serves tourists / has international guests / changes menus frequently], 
it's typically live in under 30 minutes using a photo of your existing menu.

I'd love to show you how it works in a 20-minute call. Would [day] or [day] work?

[Name]
mellow.menu
```

### Walk-In Script
"Hi, I'm [name] — I run a software platform for restaurants called mellow.menu. We give restaurants a live digital menu in 40+ languages that their guests can order and pay from. I'm looking for 5 restaurant owners to try it for free and give me feedback. Would the owner have 5 minutes this week?"

## Channel 2 — Product Hunt

**Phase:** 2 (one-time launch event)
**Target audience:** Tech-savvy restaurant owners, SaaS enthusiasts who know restaurant owners
**Messaging angle:** "We built the digital menu tool that's actually smart"
**Expected CAC:** EUR 0 (time investment only)
**Effort:** Medium (intensive for 1 week)

### Strategy
Product Hunt is one of the best free launch channels for B2B SaaS. The goal is not to sell directly — it's to generate awareness, social proof (being a top product of the day), and inbound traffic. A strong Product Hunt launch drives backlinks, press coverage, and trial signups from people who would never find you otherwise.

### Specific Tasks
- [ ] Create a Product Hunt account and start engaging with the community 2 weeks before launch (upvote, comment)
- [ ] Prepare all assets: tagline (50 chars), description (200 chars), 5 product screenshots, demo GIF or video, first comment from maker
- [ ] Recommended tagline: "Digital menus + AI pricing + table ordering — in 40 languages"
- [ ] Submit on a Wednesday or Thursday (avoid Monday; avoid weekends)
- [ ] Launch at 12:01 AM Pacific — this gives you the full 24-hour window
- [ ] Brief your entire network to upvote at launch time (not before midnight)
- [ ] Ask all pilot restaurant owners to leave a review
- [ ] Respond to every comment within 30 minutes during launch day

### Product Hunt First Comment (Draft)
"Hi PH! I'm [name], founder of mellow.menu.

I built this because I kept seeing tourist restaurants where 30% of guests couldn't read the menu. And I kept seeing restaurant owners reprinting menus every time prices changed, with no idea which items actually made money.

mellow.menu is the digital menu that translates itself into 40+ languages, tells you which items to push based on margin, and lets guests order and split the bill at the table.

You can import your existing menu from a photo. Setup takes 30 minutes.

Happy to answer any questions — especially from restaurant owners in the community!"

## Channel 3 — LinkedIn (Founder Personal Brand)

**Phase:** 1–3 (ongoing)
**Target audience:** Restaurant owners, hospitality operators, food service managers
**Messaging angle:** Founder-led authenticity — building in public, sharing restaurant industry insights
**Expected CAC:** EUR 50–200 (via DM conversion after content engagement)
**Effort:** Medium (2–3 posts per week)

### Strategy
LinkedIn reaches restaurant owners and operators far better than Twitter/X or Instagram because it's where business owners go to make decisions. The founder's personal profile, not the company page, should be the primary channel. People buy from people.

### Content Themes
1. **Building in public** — "What I learned from onboarding my first 5 restaurants"
2. **Restaurant industry insights** — "The 3 items on most menus that drain profit" (hooks non-customers into your content orbit)
3. **Product demos** — Short videos showing specific features in action (multilingual in 15 seconds; OCR import from a photo)
4. **Customer stories** — With permission, share specific results from pilot restaurants
5. **Controversial takes** — "Paper menus are a marketing failure" or "Most restaurant pricing is guesswork"

### Posting Schedule
- 3 posts/week: 1 insight/opinion, 1 behind-the-scenes/build-in-public, 1 product or customer story
- Comment on 10 restaurant/hospitality posts per day (build relationships, not just broadcast)
- Send 5–10 personalised connection requests per week to restaurant owners

### LinkedIn Ad Strategy (Month 3+)
- **Do not run LinkedIn Ads before Month 3.** Organic should come first.
- When ready: target Restaurant Owner + General Manager job titles, Hospitality industry, specific cities
- Use lead gen forms (pre-filled from LinkedIn profile) for demo booking
- Budget: EUR 500/month test; scale if CAC is below EUR 200

## Channel 4 — Restaurant Community and Facebook Groups

**Phase:** 1–3
**Target audience:** Independent restaurant owners, café owners, hospitality entrepreneurs
**Messaging angle:** Peer recommendation, not advertising
**Expected CAC:** EUR 0–50
**Effort:** Medium (2–4 hours/week)

### Specific Communities to Target

| Community | Type | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Restaurant Owners UK/IRE Facebook groups | Facebook | Search "restaurant owners UK", "restaurant owners Ireland" — large active groups |
| r/restaurantowners (Reddit) | Reddit | High-quality discussion; provide genuine value before promoting |
| Independents by Design | Association | UK independent restaurant network |
| Restaurants Association of Ireland | Association | Ireland |
| National Restaurant Association (NRA) | Association | US market for Phase 3 |
| Local hospitality WhatsApp groups | WhatsApp | Hard to find but extremely valuable; ask pilot restaurants to introduce you |
| Hospitality professionals LinkedIn groups | LinkedIn | Search "restaurant owners group" |

### Approach
- Do not spam these groups. Join, observe for one week, then contribute useful content (answer questions, share non-promotional insights)
- After establishing presence, share the case study as a discussion post: "I built something for restaurants like yours — here's what happened"
- Offer free setup calls to group members (limited to 5 per month)
- Monitor for people asking "how do you handle international guests?" or "what digital menu tool do you use?" — these are buying signals, engage immediately

## Channel 5 — SEO and Content Marketing

**Phase:** 3 (foundations in Phase 2, traffic in Phase 3+)
**Target audience:** Restaurant owners searching for solutions
**Messaging angle:** Educational authority on restaurant technology and profitability
**Expected CAC:** EUR 50–150 at maturity (12+ months)
**Effort:** High (requires consistent investment over 6–12 months)

### Why SEO Is a Phase 3 Play
SEO takes 6–12 months to generate meaningful organic traffic. Start the foundations in Phase 2 (technical SEO, schema markup, site structure), but do not expect results before Month 6. The content strategy should be started in Month 3 so it compounds into a lead generation engine by Month 9–12.

### Priority Keyword Clusters

| Cluster | Example Keywords | Intent | Difficulty |
|---|---|---|---|
| Digital menu tools | "digital menu for restaurants", "QR code menu software", "online menu builder" | Commercial | High |
| Restaurant technology | "restaurant management software", "restaurant ordering system" | Commercial | High |
| Menu multilingual | "multilingual restaurant menu", "restaurant menu translation" | Commercial | Low–Medium |
| Restaurant pricing | "menu pricing strategy", "restaurant profit margin by dish" | Informational | Low |
| Bill splitting | "restaurant bill splitting app", "table ordering app" | Commercial | Low–Medium |
| OCR menu import | "import menu from photo", "digitise paper menu" | Commercial | Very Low |

**Recommendation:** Start with the low-competition long-tail clusters first ("multilingual restaurant menu software", "how to digitise a restaurant menu") where you can rank in 3–6 months. Build content that earns links and establishes authority. Attack the high-competition clusters in Year 2.

### Content Calendar (Month 3–6)

| Month | Post Title | Target Keyword | Format |
|---|---|---|---|
| M3 | "How we helped a Dublin restaurant serve guests in 12 languages" | case study + multilingual | Case study |
| M3 | "The restaurant owner's guide to menu pricing in 2026" | restaurant pricing strategy | Guide |
| M4 | "How to import your paper menu into a digital format in 5 minutes" | digitise paper menu | Tutorial |
| M4 | "Why your QR code menu might be costing you sales" | QR code menu problems | Opinion |
| M5 | "Restaurant menu engineering: the items you should cut, price higher, and promote" | menu engineering | Guide |
| M5 | "How bill splitting works (and why it matters for table turnover)" | restaurant bill splitting | Explainer |
| M6 | "Digital menu ROI: what restaurants actually save and earn" | digital menu ROI | Data post |

### Technical SEO Foundations (Phase 2)
- [ ] Ensure all marketing pages have unique title tags and meta descriptions
- [ ] Implement schema.org/Organization and schema.org/SoftwareApplication on homepage
- [ ] Build an XML sitemap and submit to Google Search Console
- [ ] Implement canonical tags on all pages
- [ ] **Flag for engineering:** Public menu pages need schema.org/Menu structured data — see 06_tech_roadmap_inputs.md

## Channel 6 — Partnerships

**Phase:** 2–3
**Target audience:** Reached via partner's existing customer base
**Messaging angle:** Complement, not compete — "mellow.menu works with your existing tools"
**Expected CAC:** EUR 100–300 (shared with partner)
**Effort:** Medium (relationship-building takes time but scales well)

### Priority Partnership Types

#### A. Square and Stripe Partner Programmes
- mellow.menu already integrates both. Apply to be listed in the Square App Marketplace and Stripe Partners directory
- These are high-intent discovery channels — restaurant owners searching for tools to use with Square/Stripe
- **Action:** Apply to Square App Marketplace immediately after Phase 1
- **Action:** Apply to Stripe's App Marketplace / Solutions partners

#### B. Hospitality Consultants and Accountants
- Restaurant-focused accountants and hospitality consultants often advise clients on technology purchases
- A referral arrangement (10–20% first-year revenue share) gives them an incentive to recommend mellow.menu
- **Action:** Identify 10 hospitality-focused accountancy firms in target cities; reach out with a referral proposal
- These are high-trust referrals and typically close faster than cold leads

#### C. Menu Design Agencies and Graphic Designers
- Freelancers and agencies that design restaurant menus are a natural fit — they can offer mellow.menu as a digital publishing platform
- **Action:** Build a simple partner page on mellow.menu explaining the referral offer
- **Action:** Reach out to 20 menu design freelancers (find on Behance, Dribbble, Upwork)

#### D. Hospitality Associations
- Becoming a "recommended supplier" or "technology partner" with national restaurant associations provides instant credibility
- **Action:** Apply to the Restaurants Association of Ireland and UK Hospitality supplier programmes
- These often require a small membership fee but give access to directories and conference speaking

#### E. Food Delivery Platforms (Long-term)
- Deliveroo, Uber Eats, and Just Eat serve many of the same restaurants but do not solve the in-venue experience
- Long-term: explore API integration with their merchant platforms; mellow.menu handles in-venue ordering, they handle delivery
- Not a Phase 1–2 priority; flag for Month 9+

## Channel 7 — PR and Trade Press

**Phase:** 2–3
**Target audience:** Restaurant owners who read trade publications
**Messaging angle:** "Irish startup helping restaurants serve international guests" + product innovation angle
**Expected CAC:** EUR 0–50 (time to write and pitch)
**Effort:** Medium

### Target Publications

| Publication | Type | Audience | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hospitality Ireland | Trade press | Irish restaurant owners | Strong match for Phase 2 launch |
| The Caterer (UK) | Trade press | UK hospitality operators | Largest UK hospitality trade pub |
| BigHospitality (UK) | Trade press | UK hospitality | Online-first; frequent new product coverage |
| Restaurant magazine (UK) | Trade press | Restaurant owners | Monthly print + digital |
| Food & Wine (Ireland) | Consumer/trade | Irish food industry | Human-interest angle works well |
| TechCrunch / The Next Web | Tech press | Investors + tech community | For fundraising PR, not customer acquisition |
| Local press in pilot restaurant's city | Consumer | General public | "Local restaurant launches new tech" stories are easy wins |

### PR Angle Options
1. **Innovation angle:** "Irish startup uses AI to help restaurants serve guests in 40+ languages" (for tech and national press)
2. **Tourism angle:** "New tech helps restaurants close the language barrier for international tourists" (for hospitality and tourism press)
3. **ROI angle:** "Digital menus are adding 15% to restaurant revenue — here's how" (for trade press, requires data from pilot restaurants)
4. **Local human interest:** "[Restaurant name] in [city] is the first in [area] to offer a multilingual digital menu" (for local press)

### PR Approach
- Write a 300-word press release and send to editors personally (not a PR wire)
- Personalise each pitch with a reason why it's relevant to their specific readership
- Offer an exclusive to the most relevant trade publication first
- Follow up once after 5 business days; do not pester

## Channel 8 — Referral Programme

**Phase:** 3 (Month 7+)
**Target audience:** Existing restaurant customers
**Messaging angle:** "Know another restaurant owner? They'll love mellow.menu — and you'll earn EUR 100"
**Expected CAC:** EUR 30–80
**Effort:** Low (once built)

### Strategy
Restaurant owners know other restaurant owners. A well-structured referral programme turns your happiest customers into a sales channel. This is your highest-ROI acquisition channel at scale — but it only works after you have a base of satisfied customers to activate.

### Programme Design (Recommended)
- **Incentive:** EUR 100 account credit (or cash if preferred) for every referred restaurant that stays paying for 3+ months
- **Referred restaurant:** Gets 1 month free on any plan
- **Mechanism:** Unique referral link per restaurant; tracked in the platform
- **Promotion:** Email to all customers at Month 7 launch; in-app banner; mentioned in onboarding sequence

### Technical Dependency
This requires a referral tracking system. See 06_tech_roadmap_inputs.md — flag for engineering backlog before Month 6.

## Channel 9 — Paid Search (Google Ads)

**Phase:** 3 (Month 8+)
**Target audience:** Restaurant owners actively searching for digital menu tools
**Messaging angle:** Benefit-led, specific to search intent
**Expected CAC:** EUR 150–400 (test phase)
**Effort:** Medium

### Strategy
Do not run paid search until you have:
1. A converting demo booking page (proven organic conversion rate)
2. At least 20 paying customers to validate the product
3. A monthly budget commitment of EUR 500+ to generate meaningful data

### Recommended Ad Strategy
- **Branded campaigns:** Protect "mellow.menu" search term from day one (low cost, high intent)
- **Competitor campaigns:** Target "Mr Yum alternative", "me&u alternative", "digital menu software" (medium cost)
- **Solution campaigns:** "QR menu for restaurants", "multilingual restaurant menu", "restaurant ordering app" (higher cost)

### Budget Allocation (Test Phase, Month 8)
| Campaign Type | Monthly Budget | Expected Clicks | Expected Demos |
|---|---|---|---|
| Branded | EUR 100 | 200 | 5–10 |
| Competitor terms | EUR 200 | 150 | 3–6 |
| Solution terms | EUR 200 | 100 | 2–4 |

|---|---|---|---|---|
