# Positioning, Messaging, and ICP
## mellow.menu Go-To-Market Plan — Document 01

---

## 1. Primary Positioning Statement

**mellow.menu is the all-in-one digital menu and ordering platform that helps independent and growing restaurants serve international guests, optimise their most profitable items, and run smoother table service — without needing a developer or a separate system for every problem.**

This positioning is built on three pillars that no single competitor currently owns simultaneously:
1. **Multilingual by default** (40+ languages via DeepL) — a genuine differentiator for any tourist-facing or multicultural urban market
2. **AI-powered revenue intelligence** (pricing, bundling, margin tracking) — not just a digital menu, but a menu that earns more
3. **End-to-end table experience** (QR order, bill-splitting, payments) — reduces staff friction and covers per table increase

- Real-time ordering with bill-splitting

This combination is the competitive story. Every sales conversation should return to it.

## 4. ICP Definition

### Tier 1 — Primary ICP (Target First)

**Independent Restaurant Owner, Tourist District or Urban Multicultural Market**

- **Size:** 30–150 covers, 1 location
- **Type:** Casual dining, café, bistro, wine bar
- **Geography:** Cities with significant international tourist traffic or large immigrant communities (London, Dublin, Amsterdam, Lisbon, Sydney, NYC, Melbourne)
- **Tech maturity:** Has a smartphone and uses Square or Stripe already; may have a static QR code but not a live digital menu
- **Revenue:** EUR/GBP/AUD 500k–3M annually
- **Decision maker:** Owner-operator, or owner + one manager
- **Pain points:**
  - Reprints menus every time prices or items change — expensive and slow
  - Non-English-speaking guests struggle with the menu; staff spend time explaining
  - No idea which menu items are most profitable
  - Paying for separate tools (reservations, payment, menu) that don't talk to each other
- **Buying trigger:** A slow season, a menu redesign, a price increase, or seeing a competitor with a better digital menu
- **Expected CAC:** EUR 80–200 (founder-led outbound or referral)
- **Expected LTV:** EUR 1,500–6,000 (Starter/Pro plan, 12–36 month retention)

### Tier 2 — Secondary ICP (Prioritise After Traction)

**Multi-Location Casual Chain, 2–10 Sites**

- **Size:** 2–10 locations, 50–200 covers each
- **Type:** Fast casual, café chain, pizza/burger group
- **Geography:** Same as above, but national rather than purely local
- **Tech maturity:** Currently using inconsistent menu tools across locations; may have a basic POS
- **Decision maker:** Operations Manager or Head of Marketing, with founder/CEO sign-off
- **Pain points:**
  - Inconsistent menus and pricing across locations
  - No centralised reporting on what sells and what doesn't
  - Staff training burden when menus change
- **Expected CAC:** EUR 300–800 (outbound + demo)
- **Expected LTV:** EUR 6,000–24,000 (Business plan, multi-seat, 24+ month retention)

### Tier 3 — Aspirational (Do Not Target at Launch)

**Enterprise restaurant groups, franchises, hotel F&B** — requires custom contracts, integrations, SLAs, and dedicated support. Do not pursue before Month 6 unless inbound.

## 5. Buyer Persona Profiles

### Persona A: "Marco the Independent Owner"
- Age 38–55, runs a 70-cover Italian restaurant in a tourist neighbourhood
- Works 60+ hours/week in the business
- Makes all purchasing decisions himself
- Is suspicious of tech — has been burned by complex POS systems
- Responds to: peer recommendations, demonstrations, simplicity messaging, "set up in 30 minutes"
- Will not respond to: enterprise jargon, long-term lock-in, complex pricing

### Persona B: "Siobhan the Ops Manager"
- Age 28–42, manages 3–5 locations for a growing café group
- Reports to the founder; has budget authority up to EUR 500/month
- Evaluates tools based on: consistency, reporting, support quality, onboarding speed
- Responds to: ROI framing, comparison tables, integration reliability, case studies from similar businesses
- Will not respond to: consumer-style marketing, feature lists without business context

## 7. Objection Handling Scripts

### "We already have a QR code menu."
"That's a great start — but a static QR code is just a PDF. mellow.menu is a live system: you update prices once and every table sees it instantly. Guests can actually order and pay through it, which reduces how often your staff walk back and forth taking orders. And it translates itself — so the tourist who only speaks French or Japanese can read your full menu without needing a paper translation."

### "We can't afford another monthly subscription."
"I understand. What's it costing you right now to print menus every time something changes? Most restaurants we work with spend EUR 200–400 a year on reprints alone — our Starter plan is less than that. And if even one table per week orders an extra item because the AI told you to bundle your best-margin side dish with your main, that more than covers the cost."

### "Our guests prefer paper menus."
"You don't have to eliminate paper — most of our restaurants run both for the first few months. What you get immediately is the multilingual version your international guests love, and the ordering flow your staff love because it reduces errors and back-and-forth. Paper becomes a choice, not a necessity."

### "I'm worried about setup complexity."
"The most common thing people say after setting up mellow.menu is 'that was faster than I expected.' You can import your existing menu by taking a photo with your phone — our system reads it and builds the digital menu automatically. Most restaurants are live in under 30 minutes. And we give you a dedicated onboarding session to make sure everything looks right before you go live."

### "We're locked into our current POS."
"mellow.menu isn't a POS replacement — it sits on top of whatever you're already using. We support Stripe and Square for payments, and we don't require you to rip out anything existing. Most restaurants use us alongside their current till system."

### "What if the internet goes down?"
"Great question — your QR code links to our hosted menu which loads fast on mobile even on poor connections. For ordering, we recommend having a simple backup paper pad for genuine outages, but in practice we have 99.9% uptime and most venues never need it. Your existing till system is unaffected regardless."

## 8. Brand Voice and Tone

- **Warm, not corporate.** We talk to restaurant owners like a knowledgeable friend in the industry, not like enterprise software vendors.
- **Confident, not arrogant.** We make specific claims backed by product capability. We do not overpromise.
- **Simple, not dumbed down.** Owners are smart business people. Respect their intelligence while keeping language clear.
- **Direct.** Lead with the benefit, not the feature. "Translate your menu automatically" not "DeepL-powered localisation engine."
